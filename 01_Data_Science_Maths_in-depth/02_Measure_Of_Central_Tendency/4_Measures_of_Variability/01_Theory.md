# Mean Absolute Deviation
The mean absolute deviation of a dataset is the average distance between each data point and the mean. 
It gives us an idea about the variability in a dataset.

$$
MAD = \frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})}{n}
$$

# Standard Deviation
The standard deviation is a measure of the amount of variation or dispersion of a set of values. 
A low standard deviation insicates that the values tend to be close to the mean (also called the expected value) of the set, while a high standard deviation indicates that the values are spread out over a wider range.

$$
\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i -\mu)^2}{N}}
$$

where:
$\sigma = population standard deviation$  
$N = the size of the population$  
$x_i = each value from the population$  
$\mu = the population mean$  

# Variance
Variance is a measure of how data points differ from the mean.
According to Layman, a variance is a measure of how far a set of data(numbers) are spread out from their mean(average) value.

$$
\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum (s_i - \mu)^2}{N}
$$
