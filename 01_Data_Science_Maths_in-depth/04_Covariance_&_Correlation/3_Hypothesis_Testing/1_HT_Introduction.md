# Hypothesis Testing
It is a part of statistical analysis, where we test the assumptions made regarding a population parameter.
It is generally used when we were to compare a single group with an external standard and two or more groups with each other.

## Terminology used in Hypothesis Testing
### - Null Hypothesis:
Null hypothesis is a statistical theory that suggests there is no statistical significance exists between the populations.
It is denoted by HO and read as H-naught.

### - Alternative Hypothesis: 
An Alternative hypothesis suggests there is a significant difference between the population parameters. It could be greater or smaller.
Basically, it is the contrast of the Null Hypothesis. It is denoted by Ha or H1.

# Common Hypothesis Testing

- t test

## methord to solve
### Steps of Hypothesis Testing:
- State null(Ho) and alternative(H1) hypothesis
- Choose level of significance($\alpha$)
- Find critical values
- Find test statistic
- Draw your conclusion
